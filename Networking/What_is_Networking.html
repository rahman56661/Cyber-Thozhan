What is a Network?

Understanding how devices connect and communicate

Definition

A network is formed when two or more devices (phones, laptops, routers, etc.) connect together to share data and communicate.

Simple explanation: When data travels from one device to another device, that's a network.

Use Cases - Simple & Clear

File Sharing

Transfer files between devices that are on the same network.

Example: Sending a file from your phone to a friend's phone using Hotspot, Wi-Fi, or Bluetooth.

Internet Access

Connecting devices to the internet through a network.

Example: Your phone or laptop connecting to a home router to access websites.

Communication

Sending messages and making calls through networks.

Example: WhatsApp messages, Instagram DMs, emails - all travel through networks.

Resource Sharing

Sharing devices and resources across multiple computers.

Example: Multiple computers in an office sharing one printer, scanner, or shared folders.
