Metropolitan Area Network (MAN)

Connecting Local Networks Across Cities

MAN = Metropolitan Area Network

A network that covers a larger area than a LAN but smaller than a WAN.

Simple Explanation

A Metropolitan Area Network connects multiple Local Area Networks across a city or metropolitan area.

LAN

Small area coverage (home, office, building)

MAN

City or metropolitan area coverage

WAN

Country or global coverage

MAN serves as an intermediate network level between LAN and WAN.

MAN Coverage Area

Approximately: 5 km to 50 km

Can cover an entire city, groups of buildings, industrial areas, or IT parks.

MAN Examples (Real World)

City-wide Internet Network

Internet service providers like Comcast, Spectrum, or AT&T deploying broadband infrastructure across a metropolitan area.

Metro Rail Wi-Fi

Seamless wireless network covering entire metro rail systems and stations across a city.

University Campus Network

Connecting multiple campus buildings, libraries, and administrative centers across a university town.

Government Office Network

Interconnecting various government buildings like city hall, courts, and public service offices within a metropolitan area.

Cable TV Network

Distribution network for cable television services across a city or metropolitan region.

MAN Use Cases

  • Interconnecting multiple LANs: Connecting office networks across different locations in the same city.
  • High-speed internet distribution: ISPs using MAN infrastructure to deliver broadband services across urban areas.
  • Public Wi-Fi services: Municipal wireless networks providing internet access across city centers.
  • CCTV Surveillance systems: Connecting security cameras across a city for smart city initiatives.
  • Educational networks: Connecting schools and educational institutions within a district.

MAN Advantages & Disadvantages

Advantages

  • Larger geographical coverage than LAN
  • High-speed connectivity using fiber optics
  • Better reliability and uptime
  • Efficient interconnection of multiple LANs
  • Cost-effective for city-wide services
  • Supports high bandwidth applications

Disadvantages

  • Higher setup costs (fiber cables, networking equipment)
  • Complex maintenance requirements
  • Potential for network congestion during peak usage
  • Increased security challenges
  • Requires specialized technical expertise
  • Regulatory and right-of-way issues for infrastructure

Summary

A Metropolitan Area Network (MAN) is a large computer network that spans a metropolitan area, connecting multiple Local Area Networks (LANs). It serves as an intermediate network between LANs and Wide Area Networks (WANs).

Key Points:

  • MAN covers a larger geographical area than LAN but smaller than WAN
  • Typically spans 5-50 km, covering a city or metropolitan region
  • Uses high-speed transmission media like fiber optics
  • Enables efficient resource sharing across multiple locations
  • Commonly deployed by ISPs, governments, and large organizations

Real-world examples:

  • City-wide fiber internet networks
  • Metro area Wi-Fi systems
  • University campus networks
  • Smart city surveillance systems
  • Cable television distribution networks
