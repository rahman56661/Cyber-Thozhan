Campus Area Network (CAN)

Connecting Multiple Buildings Within a Campus Environment

What is a Campus Area Network?

A Campus Area Network (CAN) is a computer network that interconnects multiple Local Area Networks (LANs) within a limited geographical area such as a university campus, corporate campus, or any large facility with multiple buildings.

Simple Meaning

When LAN networks from multiple buildings in a campus are connected together, it forms a CAN. This allows seamless communication and resource sharing across the entire campus.

Campus Definition

A "campus" refers to any area with multiple buildings in close proximity, such as:

  • College/University campuses
  • IT parks
  • Hospital complexes
  • Military bases
  • Government complexes

Network Scale Comparison

Understanding where CAN fits in the network hierarchy:

LAN

Local Area Network

Single building or small area

Coverage: Up to 1 km

CAN

Campus Area Network

Multiple buildings in a campus

Coverage: 1-5 km (up to 10 km)

MAN

Metropolitan Area Network

City-wide network

Coverage: 5-50 km

LAN < CAN < MAN - CAN bridges the gap between building-level and city-wide networks.

CAN Coverage Area

Typically spans between 1 km to 5 km, though global standards sometimes mention coverage up to 10 km depending on the campus size and requirements.

Key Characteristics

CANs are designed to provide high-speed connectivity across multiple buildings while maintaining the performance and security benefits of a localized network.

Real-World CAN Examples

Here are common implementations of Campus Area Networks:

University Campus

Connecting various campus buildings:

  • Admin building
  • Engineering block
  • Library
  • Hostels
  • Research centers

Usage: Shared servers, campus Wi-Fi, resource sharing, centralized management systems.

IT Park Network

Examples: TIDEL Park, Manyata Tech Park, DLF IT Park

Multiple blocks (A, B, C, D) each with their own LAN, interconnected to form a CAN.

Usage: High-speed data transfer, shared resources, centralized security.

Hospital Complex

Large hospital campuses with multiple specialized buildings:

  • OP building
  • IP building
  • Emergency department
  • Radiology center
  • Pharmacy

Usage: Patient records, medical imaging, billing systems.

Government / Military Campus

Large government or military installations:

  • Police HQ
  • Data centers
  • Training facilities
  • Control rooms

Usage: Secure communications, surveillance systems, centralized command.

CAN Use Cases

Campus Area Networks serve multiple important functions:

Resource Sharing

Servers, printers, scanners and other resources can be shared across all buildings in the campus.

Campus WiFi

Seamless WiFi roaming across the entire campus with single authentication.

Data Center Access

All buildings can access centralized servers and data vaults with high-speed connectivity.

High-Speed Communication

Fiber optic cables provide near-LAN speeds across the entire campus.

Security & CCTV

Campus-wide CCTV systems connected to a central control room for comprehensive security monitoring.

Advantages & Disadvantages

Advantages of CAN

  • High-speed internal network with performance close to LAN
  • Lower cost compared to MAN/WAN implementations
  • Enhanced security as the network is limited to campus area
  • Centralized management and administration
  • Efficient resource sharing across multiple buildings
  • Scalable to accommodate campus growth

Disadvantages of CAN

  • Limited to campus geographical area
  • Initial setup costs for fiber cables and access points
  • Requires regular maintenance and administration
  • Single point of failure if central infrastructure fails
  • Can be complex to design for optimal performance
